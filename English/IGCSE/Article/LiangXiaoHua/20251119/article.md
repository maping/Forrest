Stricter Punishments Can Improve Road Safety‌

Recently our class discussed different ways to improve road safety. Some classmates believe education helps drivers build better habits, while others argue that technology, such as speed cameras, is the best solution. However, I believe stricter punishments play the most important role.

Firstly, ‌strong penalties discourage dangerous behaviour more directly‌. When people understand that danger, such as speeding or using a phone while driving, will lead to serious consequences, they are more likely to act cautiously before taking risks. This fear of punishment helps drivers stay more focused and follow signs more carefully, which reduces the chance of accidents.

Secondly, stricter rules bring faster results. When a new law with heavier penalties is introduced, drivers usually change their behaviour immediately. They quickly realise the risks and adjust their habits, which leads to fewer accidents in a short period of time. This quick improvement makes stricter punishments an effective method for road safety.

In conclusion, while education and technology contribute to road safety as well, tougher punishments provide both immediate and reliable results, making them crucial methods for protecting all road users.
